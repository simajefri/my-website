I am an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science at University of North Carolina Wilmington. I received my Ph.D. in Mathematics and Computer Science. My research interest lies in a spectrum of topics in security and privacy, including but not limited to cybersecurity defense (scam, fraud and social engineering defense), cryptography protocol design for applications, and the application of AI in security.

Research & Publications

Research Areas

Cybersecurity Defense

Focusing on scam, fraud, and social engineering defense mechanisms. Current research includes analyzing social engineering techniques in software supply chain attacks and developing countermeasures.

Cryptography Protocol Design

Designing efficient protocols for multi-party computation, homomorphic secret sharing, and publicly evaluatable perceptual hashing for real-world security applications.

AI in Security

Exploring the application of artificial intelligence and machine learning in cybersecurity, including vulnerability assessment and threat detection.
